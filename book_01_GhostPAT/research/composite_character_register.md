# Composite Character Register

Book: **Ghosts of Trust**
Purpose: prevent protected composites from being mistaken for identifiable real people.

## Rules

1. A composite exists to protect identity, compress chronology, or represent a documented pattern—not to manufacture evidence.
2. No composite may combine details in a way that falsely implicates a real person.
3. Distinctive identifying details must be altered or removed unless explicit authority exists.
4. A composite's emotional truth may be drawn from multiple source accounts, but factual claims remain attached to their individual sources.
5. Verbatim quotations are prohibited unless separately documented and authorized.
6. Survivor-derived characters require trauma-informed review before publication.

## Required review questions

Before a composite enters canon:

- What documented pattern does the character represent?
- Which source IDs support each constituent element?
- Could a reader identify a real person from the remaining details?
- Does chronology create a false factual connection?
- Does dialogue imply a quotation that was never recorded?
- Does the composite preserve dignity and agency?
- Is the scene necessary, or can the pattern be stated without a character?

## Later-book survivor rule

Epstein-era and other trafficking-survivor POVs belong in a separate survivor-source register. The youngest potential POVs must be based on the latest evidence-supported victim chronology. They may continue into later 3I/ATLAS-era books only as adults and only after identity-protection, chronology, and evidentiary review.
