## Required metadata for new sources

Every new source entry must record:

- stable source ID;
- title or neutral description;
- source class;
- author/publisher/custodian;
- creation/publication date;
- retrieval date;
- durable URI, repository path, or evidence-package pointer;
- hash when bytes are retained;
- access restrictions;
- claims supported and claims not supported;
- freshness or supersession state;
- survivor/privacy restrictions.

## Interpretation boundary

A source supports only what it actually establishes. Correlation among workstation failures, missing payments, later credential anomalies, and public fraud cases must remain thematic or investigatory unless admissible evidence establishes a causal link.
