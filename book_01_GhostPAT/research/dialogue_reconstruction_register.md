# Dialogue Reconstruction Register

Book: **Ghosts of Trust**
Purpose: prevent generated or remembered dialogue from being represented as a verbatim historical quotation.

## Dialogue classes

- `VERBATIM_DOCUMENTED`: preserved in a recording, transcript, message, note, or other durable source.
- `REMEMBERED_GIST`: author remembers the substance but not exact wording.
- `COMPOSITE_RECONSTRUCTION`: combines recurring language or multiple events.
- `FICTIONAL_CONNECTIVE`: invented solely for scene flow and not a factual claim.
- `EXCLUDE`: too sensitive, identifying, unsupported, or misleading.

## Admission requirements

Before quoted dialogue enters canon:

1. assign a stable dialogue ID;
2. identify the dialogue class;
3. record the supporting source or author confidence;
4. assess identifiability and harm;
5. choose verbatim, paraphrase, reconstructed, or omitted presentation;
6. obtain explicit approval for sensitive survivor, medical, self-harm, employment, or allegation-related material.

## Style rule

Emotional accuracy does not authorize false quotation. When exact wording is unavailable, preserve the emotional and causal function through transparent paraphrase, interior reflection, or clearly reconstructed dialogue.
