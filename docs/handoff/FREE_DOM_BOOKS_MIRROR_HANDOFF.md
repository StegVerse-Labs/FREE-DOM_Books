## 1. Canonical decisions

1. Book 1 covers Rigel Randolph's lived investigation period and ends by February 2020.
2. Ghost_PAT is a later flash-forward and origin echo. It must not imply that the VA-era investigation continued beyond February 2020.
3. Book 2 is titled **The Ledger of Silence**.
4. Epstein-era character POVs begin well before February 2020 and must be grounded in available factual evidence.
5. The youngest evidence-supported victim POVs may continue into later 3I/ATLAS-era books as adult protagonists, but age, chronology, and experience must remain evidence-constrained.
6. Survivor identities must be protected. Source-derived fact, lived memory, composite construction, and fictional connective tissue must be explicitly distinguished.
7. Chapters generated in chat remain drafts until individually reconciled against evidence and committed canon.
8. Rige1 preserves Rigel's voice, reasoning, values, and continuity while refusing unsupported invention.
9. No unsupported allegation about a real person may be presented as fact.
10. Publication or irreversible public release requires explicit user approval.

## 2. Confirmed narrative anchors

- Ghost_PAT / "The Night the Key Wouldn't Die" is the Book 1 opening anchor.
- The Waco VAMC period spans approximately 2017 through February 6, 2020.
- The I-35 side-swipe by another federal employee occurred after work and is a required memoir event, subject to factual review.
- Rigel returned to work immediately after the collision.

- "Approval is not continuity. Execution is not admissibility." remains a governing thematic principle for the wider series.

## 3. Evidence and authorship classes

Every scene, claim, and character element must be tagged internally as one of:

- `LIVED_MEMORY`: first-person memory supplied by Rigel.
- `DOCUMENTED_FACT`: supported by a durable source record.
- `PUBLIC_RECORD`: supported by citable public evidence.
- `COMPOSITE`: deliberately merged or altered to protect identity or compress chronology.
- `FICTIONAL_CONNECTIVE_TISSUE`: invented non-claim material used only to bridge verified events.
- `UNVERIFIED_LEAD`: retained for research but prohibited from canonical factual narration.
- `NONCANONICAL_DRAFT`: generated material awaiting review.

Dialogue may not be presented as verbatim unless documented or explicitly remembered with sufficient confidence. Otherwise it must be framed as reconstructed or composite.
